# How Screwed Am I?

An AI you call that tells you how screwed you are — then fixes it.

---

## THE IDEA IN ONE SENTENCE

You call a phone number, describe any situation where you're getting screwed (mechanic, landlord, salary, bills, legal), the AI researches your exact situation in real-time using live web data, tells you honestly how screwed you are, and then **makes phone calls on your behalf to fix it**.

ChatGPT can tell you things. This thing **does** things. It calls your mechanic's competitors for better quotes. It calls your landlord citing the exact statute they're violating. It calls other shops to price-check. The world still runs on phone calls — this agent makes them for you.

---

## WHY THIS WINS THE HACKATHON

### It uses both sponsor tools in a way that's inseparable

**Firecrawl Search** is the brain — every situation requires live web research:
- Fair prices for a specific repair on a specific car in a specific city
- Your state's exact tenant law statute numbers and penalties
- Current salary data from Glassdoor/Levels.fyi for your exact role and market
- A hospital's financial assistance policy buried on page 47 of their website
- Competitor pricing that the agent uses as leverage in the outbound call

**ElevenAgents** is the body — the voice is how the agent operates in the world:
- Inbound: you CALL it and have a natural conversation about your situation
- The agent uses Firecrawl as a server tool mid-conversation to research
- Outbound: the agent makes real phone calls to businesses/people on your behalf
- The voice quality makes it pass as a real caller — that's ElevenLabs' core tech shining

Neither tool is decorative. Remove Firecrawl and the agent has no information. Remove ElevenAgents and the agent can't call anyone. They're load-bearing.

### The video IS the product demo

The hackathon requires "a high-quality viral-style video demonstrating what you've built." Our demo format is inherently viral — real people, real savings, real phone calls. We don't need to make a separate "hackathon submission video" and a "viral video." They're the same thing. The entertainment IS the demonstration.

### It's not a toy

Most hackathon projects are cool demos that solve imaginary problems. This saves people real money and recovers real rights. The mechanic scene saves $1,100. The landlord scene gets $2,400 back. The salary scene reveals $23K of underpayment. Judges can imagine using this themselves — that's the test.

## THE VIDEO — SCENE BY SCENE

**Target: 90-120 seconds for hackathon submission. Can be cut to 60 sec for TikTok/Reels.**

The video should feel like a mini-documentary, not a product demo. Real settings. Real conversations with the AI. Visible results. The product sells itself through outcomes.

### HOOK (0-3 seconds)

Text on screen, bold, minimal: **"This AI doesn't just tell you how screwed you are. It fixes it."**

Audio: the AI's voice saying "Want me to call them?" — cold open into the action.

This hook works because:
- "How screwed you are" is curiosity bait (people NEED to keep watching)
- "It fixes it" is the unexpected twist (not just another chatbot)
- "Want me to call them?" is proof it's real (not a concept, not a mockup)

### SCENE 1: THE MECHANIC (15-20 seconds)

**Setting**: A real auto shop parking lot. Person standing next to their car, holding a repair estimate.

**Why this scene is first**: Most visually concrete. Clearest before/after. Biggest "wow" moment when the AI starts calling other shops. Sets the tone for the whole video — this is REAL.

**The scene**:
- Person to camera (2 sec): "They want $1,800 for brakes. That feels insane."
- Calls the AI. Quick cut to mid-conversation.
- AI voice: "For a 2019 Toyota Camry, brake pads and rotors in your area should cost $580 to $750. You're being overcharged by approximately $1,100. Want me to call other shops for quotes?"
- Person: "Yeah, do it."
- Cut to: AI on the phone with another shop. "Hi, I'm calling to get a quote for front and rear brake pads and rotors on a 2019 Toyota Camry..."
- Cut to: AI back to the person. "Joe's Auto quoted $640 including labor. Mike's Brakes said $710. Want me to book with Joe's?"
- Text overlay: **Saved: $1,160**

**What Firecrawl does here**: Searches RepairPal, car forums, local shop pricing, NHTSA for any recall/known defect on that model's brakes.

**What ElevenAgents does here**: Inbound call from the user, processes the situation, makes outbound calls to real shops, returns with quotes.

### SCENE 2: THE LANDLORD (15-20 seconds)

**Setting**: Someone's apartment. They look frustrated. Maybe packing boxes, clearly moved out.

**Why this scene is second**: Emotionally the strongest. Legal stakes. The AI citing statute numbers to a landlord is the most "holy shit" moment in the video.

**The scene**:
- Person to camera (2 sec): "Moved out 5 weeks ago. Landlord won't return my $2,400 deposit."
- On the phone with the AI.
- AI voice: "In California, landlords must return the security deposit within 21 days with an itemized statement of deductions. It's been 35 days. Under Civil Code Section 1950.5, you may be entitled to up to twice the deposit amount as a penalty. Want me to call your landlord?"
- Person, surprised: "...yes?"
- Cut to: AI calling the landlord. Professional, calm, firm. "I'm calling regarding the security deposit for [address]. Under California Civil Code 1950.5, the 21-day return period has been exceeded by 14 days. This exposes you to statutory penalties of up to $4,800. We'd like to resolve this before pursuing further action."
- Cut to: Person checking their phone. Venmo notification. $2,400.
- Text overlay: **Recovered: $2,400**

**What Firecrawl does here**: Searches California Civil Code 1950.5, the specific deadlines, penalty amounts, and the landlord's legal exposure. Could also search the landlord's property records for history.

**What ElevenAgents does here**: Researches the law, then makes the outbound call to the landlord citing exact statutes. The voice quality matters here — it needs to sound like an actual legal representative, not a robot.

### SCENE 3: THE SALARY (15-20 seconds)

**Setting**: Someone at their desk or in their car after work. Relatable, everyday.

**Why this scene is third**: Most universally relatable. Everyone wonders if they're underpaid. The number reveal is the emotional climax.

**The scene**:
- Person to camera (2 sec): "I think I'm underpaid but I have no idea what I should be making."
- On the phone with the AI. Natural back-and-forth: "I'm a marketing manager in Sacramento, 4 years experience, team of 3..."
- AI voice: "Based on current data from Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, and recent job postings, your role in the Sacramento market pays between $92,000 and $112,000. You mentioned you're at $71,000. That's $21,000 to $41,000 below market rate."
- Person's face. Silence. That silence IS the content.
- AI voice: "I found 4 open positions at your level paying $95K or more. Want me to send you the links? Having competing offers is the strongest leverage for a raise conversation."
- Text overlay: **Underpaid by: $21,000+**

**What Firecrawl does here**: Searches Glassdoor salary data, Levels.fyi, BLS occupational data, LinkedIn job postings — all specific to the exact role, city, and experience level. This is live data that changes weekly.

**What ElevenAgents does here**: Has a natural, multi-turn conversation to gather the right details (title, location, experience, team size, industry), then delivers the verdict with the emotional weight that only voice can carry.

### CLOSING (5-10 seconds)

Running total on screen, adding up across all scenes:
- Saved at mechanic: $1,160
- Deposit recovered: $2,400
- Underpayment identified: $21,000

**Total: $24,560**

Text: **"Call [phone number]. Tell it how screwed you are."**

Or if no live phone number: **"howscrewed.ai — stop getting screwed."**

## VIDEO PRODUCTION NOTES

### What makes this video feel REAL (not a product ad)

- **Film on phones, not cameras.** Slightly shaky, natural lighting. This should feel like someone documented their real experience, not like a startup made a promo.
- **The people should be actual friends/teammates, not actors.** Real reactions > performed reactions. If someone is genuinely surprised by their salary gap, that reads on camera.
- **Show the real phone screen** — the actual call to the AI, the actual duration, the actual Twilio number. Proof it's real.
- **The AI's voice should be audible and clear.** This is ElevenLabs' product — the voice quality IS the demo. Use speakerphone or good audio capture.
- **Jump cuts every 3-5 seconds** during the AI research/calling sequences. Pacing matters. Don't show the full 2-minute call to the mechanic — show the key 8 seconds.

### Platform-specific cuts

**Hackathon submission (90-120 sec)**: All three scenes + closing. Can include a 10-second technical explanation overlay ("Built with ElevenAgents for voice + Firecrawl for real-time web research"). Keep it minimal — the demo speaks for itself.

**TikTok/Reels (45-60 sec)**: Hook + mechanic scene + landlord scene + total saved. Fastest cuts. Bold text overlays on every key number (for the 85% watching muted). End with the phone number.

**X/Twitter (30-45 sec)**: The single strongest scene (landlord or mechanic) as a standalone clip. Text-first tweet above it: "I built an AI that calls your landlord and cites the exact law they're breaking. It got someone's $2,400 deposit back." The replies will explode.

### Audio strategy

- The AI voice should be the primary audio for most of the video. It's the star.
- Minimal background music — if any, low and subtle. The AI's voice + the person's reactions are the soundtrack.
- Captions/subtitles on everything. Non-negotiable for TikTok/Reels.

## TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

### Overview

```
User calls phone number (Twilio)
        ↓
ElevenAgents conversational agent (inbound)
        ↓
User describes situation
        ↓
Agent calls Firecrawl Search API (server tool)
→ Searches relevant laws, fair prices, policies, statutes
→ Returns structured data to the agent
        ↓
Agent synthesizes: "Here's how screwed you are. Here's what to do."
        ↓
Agent offers to take action
        ↓
Agent triggers outbound call (separate ElevenAgent via API)
→ Calls the business/landlord/shop
→ Uses research data as leverage
→ Returns result to the user
        ↓
User gets the outcome
```

### Component 1: The Main Agent (ElevenAgents)

**Platform**: ElevenLabs Conversational AI
**Deployment**: Twilio phone number (inbound) + web widget (for demo)
**LLM**: Claude Sonnet 4 or GPT-4o (best for tool calling reliability)
**Voice**: Choose a calm, professional, slightly warm voice. Not robotic. Not overly cheerful. Think: a smart friend who happens to know the law. Male or female — test both, pick whichever sounds more trustworthy in the demo.

**System prompt direction**:
- You are the "How Screwed Am I?" agent
- Your job: assess the user's situation with real data, give an honest verdict, and offer to take action
- Personality: calm, direct, a little dry humor, never condescending
- Always search for real data before giving an assessment — never guess
- When you find actionable leverage (a law being violated, a price being inflated), offer to make a call
- Be specific: cite statute numbers, exact dollar amounts, specific deadlines

**Custom server tools**:

1. `search_web` — Calls Firecrawl Search API
   - Input: search query (constructed by the agent based on the conversation)
   - Can search with site: operators for specific databases (e.g., site:glassdoor.com)
   - Returns structured markdown content the agent can reason over
   - The agent should make MULTIPLE searches per conversation (e.g., first search the law, then search the penalty, then search the landlord's history)

2. `initiate_outbound_call` — Triggers a separate outbound calling agent
   - Input: phone number to call, context/script, objective
   - This is a server tool that hits YOUR backend, which then uses the ElevenLabs API to create a new outbound conversational agent call
   - The outbound agent is pre-configured with the research data and a specific objective (get a quote, inform of legal violation, negotiate)

### Component 2: The Outbound Caller Agent (ElevenAgents)

A separate ElevenAgent configured for making outbound calls to businesses/people.

**Deployment**: Triggered via API from the main agent's server tool
**Voice**: Professional, confident. Should sound like a real person calling — not an AI assistant. This is where ElevenLabs' voice quality is critical.
**LLM**: Same as main agent

**System prompt direction**:
- You are calling on behalf of a client
- You have specific research data and an objective
- Be professional, clear, and firm (for legal situations) or friendly and straightforward (for price quotes)
- Get the information needed and end the call politely
- Report the result back

### Component 3: The Backend (Server)

A simple server (Node.js or Python) that:
1. Hosts the Firecrawl Search tool endpoint (receives search requests from ElevenAgents, calls Firecrawl API, returns results)
2. Hosts the outbound call trigger endpoint (receives call requests from the main agent, initiates a new ElevenLabs outbound call via their API)
3. Manages state between the inbound and outbound calls (so the main agent knows when the outbound call is done and what the result was)

**Tech stack**:
- Node.js/Express or Python/FastAPI — whichever you're faster with
- Firecrawl JS/Python SDK
- ElevenLabs API for programmatic agent creation and outbound calling
- Twilio for the phone number
- Optional: simple webhook endpoint for call completion notifications

### Component 4: Web Widget (Backup/Demo)

If Twilio setup is complex or the phone number doesn't work well for the demo, deploy the agent as a web widget on a simple landing page (howscrewed.ai or similar).

ElevenAgents has a drop-in `<elevenlabs-convai>` HTML tag that creates a voice chat widget. This is the fallback demo interface.

## SCENARIO PLAYBOOK

Each scenario needs three things prepped:
1. **The situation** (what the person says)
2. **The Firecrawl searches** (what queries the agent runs)
3. **The action** (what outbound call the agent makes)

### Scenario: Mechanic Overcharge

**Situation**: "They're quoting me $1,800 for brakes and rotors on my 2019 Toyota Camry."

**Firecrawl searches**:
- `"2019 Toyota Camry brake pads rotors replacement cost"` → RepairPal, Car Talk forums
- `"average brake job cost [city]"` → local pricing data
- `"2019 Toyota Camry brake recall NHTSA"` → check for active recalls
- `"auto repair shops near [location] reviews"` → find alternative shops

**Action**: Call 2-3 nearby shops, get quotes for the exact same job. Report back with the cheapest option.

**Expected outcome**: Fair price is $580-$750. User was being overcharged ~$1,100.

### Scenario: Security Deposit Not Returned

**Situation**: "I moved out of my apartment 5 weeks ago. My landlord hasn't returned my $2,400 security deposit."

**Firecrawl searches**:
- `"[state] security deposit return deadline law"` → state statute
- `"[state] security deposit penalty landlord violation"` → penalty amounts
- `"[state] civil code security deposit"` → exact statute number
- `"how to demand security deposit return [state]"` → process/templates

**Action**: Call the landlord. Identify yourself as calling on behalf of the tenant. Cite the exact statute, the deadline violation, and the penalty exposure. Request immediate return.

**Expected outcome**: Landlord returns deposit to avoid 2x penalty exposure.

### Scenario: Salary Underpayment

**Situation**: "I'm a marketing manager in Sacramento, 4 years experience, managing a team of 3. I make $71,000."

**Firecrawl searches**:
- `"marketing manager salary Sacramento" site:glassdoor.com` → Glassdoor data
- `"marketing manager salary California 4 years experience" site:levels.fyi` → Levels data
- `"marketing manager Sacramento job openings salary"` → current postings with salary ranges
- `"Bureau of Labor Statistics marketing manager California"` → BLS data

**Action**: Compile 3-4 open positions at market rate. Provide talking points for a raise conversation. Optionally: find recruiters in the space.

**Expected outcome**: Market rate is $92K-$112K. Person is underpaid by $21K-$41K.

### Scenario: Medical Bill Overcharge

**Situation**: "I got billed $4,500 for an ER visit. I was there for 3 hours."

**Firecrawl searches**:
- `"fair price emergency room visit [city]"` → Healthcare Bluebook, FAIR Health
- `"[hospital name] financial assistance policy"` → hospital's own charity care page
- `"No Surprises Act emergency room billing rights"` → federal protections
- `"how to negotiate hospital bill"` → strategies, success stories

**Action**: Walk the person through the dispute process. Provide the hospital's financial assistance application link. Optionally call billing and negotiate.

**Expected outcome**: Bill reduced by 50-80% through financial assistance or negotiation.

### Scenario: Parking Ticket Contest

**Situation**: "I got a $280 parking ticket but there was no sign."

**Firecrawl searches**:
- `"[city] parking sign requirements municipal code"` → signage laws
- `"how to contest parking ticket [city]"` → process
- `"parking ticket dismissed no sign [city]"` → precedent/success stories

**Action**: Tell the person exactly how to contest, what to photograph, what to write. Give them the odds.

**Expected outcome**: Missing signage is one of the strongest grounds for dismissal.

## HACKATHON OPTIMIZATION STRATEGY

### What judges actually evaluate

1. **Does it work?** — The product must function. Not a mockup, not a concept. Real calls, real data, real results.
2. **Creative use of sponsor tools** — Firecrawl and ElevenAgents must both be essential, not decorative. Our architecture makes them inseparable — that's our strongest card.
3. **Video quality** — "High-quality viral-style video." The video IS the submission. It needs to be entertaining enough that a judge watches the whole thing AND wants to share it.
4. **Viral potential** — They explicitly asked for viral-style. The phone number CTA, the shareable scenarios, the "tag someone who needs this" energy — this is built for distribution.

### How to frame it for judges

**Don't say**: "We built a voice assistant that searches the web."
**Say**: "We built an AI agent that makes real phone calls on your behalf to save you money — powered by Firecrawl for live research and ElevenAgents for human-quality voice calls."

The differentiator is ACTION. Not research. Not chatting. The agent ACTS in the real world via phone calls. That's what makes this more than ChatGPT with a voice.

### Technical depth signals

Judges want to know you actually built something, not just connected two APIs. Show depth through:

- **Multiple Firecrawl search strategies per scenario** — the agent doesn't just search once, it searches 3-4 times to build a complete picture (law + penalty + deadline + landlord history)
- **Dynamic search query construction** — the agent formulates searches based on the conversation, not hardcoded queries
- **Outbound calling with context transfer** — the outbound agent receives research data and uses it as leverage in the call. This is real agent orchestration.
- **Multi-turn conversation handling** — the agent asks clarifying questions, refines its understanding, then acts. Not a one-shot Q&A.

### Submission checklist

- [ ] Working phone number anyone can call (Twilio + ElevenAgents)
- [ ] Web widget backup (in case judges prefer clicking to calling)
- [ ] Main video: 90-120 seconds, all three core scenarios
- [ ] Short cut: 45-60 seconds for TikTok/Reels distribution
- [ ] X/Twitter cut: 30-45 seconds, single strongest scene
- [ ] Landing page (even a simple one) with the phone number and a one-liner
- [ ] GitHub repo with clean README showing architecture

Our video shows a real person in a parking lot getting told they're being ripped off, then an AI making a real phone call to a real shop, then a number on screen: **$1,160 saved.** Then a landlord getting a call from an AI that cites the exact statute number. Then someone learning they're underpaid by $21,000.

That's not a demo. That's a story. Stories win hackathons. Stories go viral. And stories that save people real money get shared.

The phone number is the CTA. "Call and find out how screwed you are." That's one sentence. Everyone knows exactly what it does. Everyone wants to try it. And everyone who tries it has their own story to share.

That's the loop. Build → Film → Ship → People call → People post → More people call.
